Reviews

In-depth thoughts on albums and songs

SCALING 1–10

THE LIFE OF A SHOW GIRL

Taylor Swift • 2025

Published: March 2025

This album leans into a softer pop-rock sound, focusing heavily on introspection. The lyrical content feels more mature compared to previous releases, with strong emphasis on identity, fame, and emotional burnout. It doesn't try to be a stadium record — and that restraint is exactly what makes it work.

Production-wise, it stays minimal but effective. Tracks are layered just enough to support the storytelling without overpowering it. While some songs feel slightly repetitive mid-album, the overall cohesion is impressive for a project this ambitious in concept.

9.2

SOUR

Olivia Rodrigo • 2021

Published: February 2025

A raw and emotionally driven debut that captures the chaos of young heartbreak with surprising clarity. The album balances pop-punk energy with stripped-down ballads in a way that never feels forced — each mode serves a different emotional moment in the narrative.

The writing can be repetitive in places, but its honesty more than compensates. Rodrigo sounds genuinely lived-in on every track, which is rare for a debut record.

8.5

21

Adele • 2011

Published: April 2025

A masterclass in heartbreak storytelling. Adele channels raw pain into soaring vocals and timeless production. Songs like "Someone Like You" and "Rolling in the Deep" became instant classics, but the deep cuts hold just as much emotional weight.

The album's cohesion is remarkable — every track earns its place, building a narrative that feels both personal and universal. It's the kind of record that becomes a lifelong companion through grief and healing.

9.2

SHORT N' SWEET

Sabrina Carpenter • 2024

Published: January 2025

A playful, confident pop album that embraces its own lightness without sacrificing substance. The songwriting is sharp and witty, blending modern production with throwback melodies. It's an album that knows exactly what it wants to be — and executes it perfectly.

While some tracks feel purely made for radio, the best moments reveal a genuine artistic point of view. This is pop music that isn't afraid to be fun, and that's exactly what makes it so refreshing.

8.8

DOO-WOPS & HOOLIGANS

Bruno Mars • 2010

Published: February 2025

An effortless blend of pop, R&B, soul, and reggae that announced Bruno Mars as a generational talent. The melodies are instantly catchy, the production is warm and timeless, and the charisma is undeniable.

Though a few ballads feel like they play it too safe, the energy and charm of tracks like "Just the Way You Are" and "Grenade" make this a debut that still sounds fresh more than a decade later.

8.7
